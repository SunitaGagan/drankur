Neck Pain

Understanding the Causes of Stiffness, Radiating Pain, and Limited Mobility

Overview

Neck pain is a common condition that can significantly impact daily life—whether it's sharp discomfort after an injury, stiffness from poor posture, or radiating pain caused by nerve irritation. At Restore Spine and Pain, we specialize in identifying and treating the full spectrum of neck-related conditions, using personalized, non-surgical strategies to help you regain comfort and function.

Neck pain may be localized or may travel into the shoulders, arms, or even the head. It can stem from joints, discs, muscles, or nerves—and finding the exact cause is the first step toward relief.

Common Symptoms

  • Stiffness or aching in the neck and upper shoulders
  • Pain that worsens with movement or prolonged sitting
  • Radiating pain into the arms or hands
  • Headaches that start at the base of the skull
  • Muscle tightness, burning, or sharp nerve-like pain

Conditions We Treat

  • Cervical Radiculopathy (Pinched Nerve in the Neck)
  • Cervical Facet Syndrome (Arthritic Neck Pain)
  • Cervicogenic Headache
  • Whiplash-Associated Disorders
  • Myofascial Pain Syndrome
  • Occipital Neuralgia

These conditions may result from injury, repetitive strain, poor posture, or degenerative changes—and often overlap, requiring expert evaluation and targeted treatment.

Our Approach

  1. Comprehensive physical exam and imaging review
  2. Conservative care focused on posture, movement, and muscle balance
  3. Minimally invasive injections to reduce inflammation or calm irritated nerves
  4. Neuromodulation techniques for chronic or treatment-resistant cases
  5. Collaborative care when needed with physical therapy, neurology, or spine surgeon
