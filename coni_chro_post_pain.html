Chronic Postoperative Pain

Persistent Pain That Continues After Surgery Has Healed

Overview

Chronic postoperative pain refers to pain that lasts for more than 3 months after a surgical procedure, even after the incision and tissues have physically healed. It can occur after many types of surgeriesâ€”including abdominal, breast, orthopedic, or hernia proceduresâ€”and is often due to nerve irritation, inflammation, or scar tissue. This pain is not a normal part of healing and should be evaluated if it interferes with daily life.

Common Symptoms

  • Ongoing pain near or around the surgical site
  • Burning, tingling, or sharp sensations
  • Sensitivity to touch or pressure in the area
  • Pain that worsens with movement, stretching, or certain positions
  • Discomfort that has lasted beyond expected recovery time

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Nerve injury or entrapment during surgery
  • Scar tissue affecting surrounding nerves or muscles
  • Repeated strain or poor postural mechanics after healing
  • Pre-existing nerve sensitivity or chronic pain conditions

How We Can Help

At Restore Spine and Pain, we understand that lingering postoperative pain can be both physically and emotionally taxing. We offer a compassionate and strategic approach to identify contributing factors and reduce pain using minimally invasive, non-opioid techniques.

Treatment Options May Include

  1. Physical therapy focused on restoring mobility and reducing tension
  2. Topical agents and non-opioid medications for nerve sensitivity
  3. Diagnostic nerve blocks to determine pain origin
  4. Image-guided trigger point or peripheral nerve injections
  5. Peripheral nerve stimulation (PNS) for persistent, focal nerve pain
  6. Spinal cord stimulation (SCS) in cases with widespread or neuropathic pain
