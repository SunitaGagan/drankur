Dr. Ankur Patel is a double board-certified interventional pain specialist with a mission to help patients relieve pain and restore function using the most advanced and personalized care available. Dr. Patel graduated from Edward Via Virginia College of Osteopathic Medicine (VCOM) at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, VA. He then completed his residency in Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation at the prestigious NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital â€“ Columbia University and Weill Cornell Medical Center, followed by a highly competitive fellowship in Pain Medicine through the Weill Cornell Tri-Institutional Pain Program, which includes Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center and Hospital for Special Surgery.

Dr. Patel specializes in treating complex spine, nerve, joint, and musculoskeletal pain using image-guided procedures, neuromodulation, and regenerative therapies. His clinical approach blends academic expertise with compassionate, individualized care. He has published extensively in peer-reviewed journals and textbooks, particularly in the field of neuromodulation and regenerative medicineâ€”where his ongoing research focuses on evidence-based applications of biologics to promote tissue healing and reduce chronic pain.

Beyond clinical practice, Dr. Patel is deeply committed to mentorship and medical education. He has held national leadership roles and serves as clinical faculty at Edward Via College of Osteopathic Medicine (VCOM), where he mentors students and residents pursuing careers in physical medicine and rehabilitation and pain medicine. He has also been invited to serve as faculty at numerous regional and national medical conferences, where he lectures on innovations in interventional pain medicine.

Dr. Patel believes in making advanced, university-level care accessible in the community. Patients value his thoughtful, evidence-driven treatment plans and his commitment to empowering them on their path to recovery.

Outside of work, Dr. Patel enjoys life in North Carolina with his wife and children. He finds joy in cooking, traveling with his family, and staying active outdoors.
