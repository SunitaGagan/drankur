Lumbar Epidural Steroid Injection

(Fluoroscopic-Guided Injection to Reduce Lower Back and Leg Pain)

Overview

A lumbar epidural steroid injection (LESI) is a minimally invasive procedure used to relieve lower back and leg pain caused by inflammation or compression of spinal nerves. This type of pain often stems from conditions like herniated discs, spinal stenosis, or degenerative disc disease.

The procedure involves injecting corticosteroid medication into the epidural space in the lumbar spine to reduce nerve inflammation and improve symptoms.

This type of pain often radiates from the neck into the shoulder, arm, or hand and may be caused by disc herniation, spinal stenosis, or degenerative changes in the spine.

The injection delivers corticosteroid medication directly into the epidural spaceâ€”the area surrounding the spinal cord and nerve rootsâ€”to reduce inflammation and nerve irritation.

This treatment is typically recommended when symptoms do not improve with conservative care such as physical therapy, medications, or activity modification.

Conditions commonly treated with LESI

  • Lumbar radiculopathy (sciatica)
  • Lumbar disc herniation
  • Lumbar spinal stenosis
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Post-laminectomy syndrome (Failed Back Surgery Syndrome)
  • Nerve pain related to auto or workplace injuries

Pre-Procedural Instructions

How Should I Prepare?

  • Driver Required: We request you to arrange for someone to drive you home.
  • Food: You may eat a light meal 1â€“2 hours before the procedure. If sedation is planned, please follow the sedation protocol provided.

Medications

  • Continue regular medications unless advised otherwise.
  • Blood thinners, NSAIDs, aspirin, and supplements: Inform us if you are taking any blood thinners (e.g., Eliquis, Plavix, Xarelto), NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen, naproxen), aspirin, or supplements with blood-thinning properties.
  • These may need to be stopped temporarily before your injection.
  • Consult your primary care physician to confirm it is safe to hold these medications.
  • Our team will guide you based on your medication list and coordinate as needed.
  • Notify us if:
    • You have a fever, infection, or are feeling unwell.
    • You are taking antibiotics.
    • Your medication list has recently changed.

Sedation

Most lumbar epidural injections are performed without sedation. However, if you have anxiety or difficulty lying flat, mild sedation may be considered at the discretion of your physician.

Special Instructions for Diabetic Patients

  • Steroid injections may temporarily raise blood sugar levels.
  • Contact your primary care provider or endocrinologist before the procedure for guidance on medication adjustments.
  • Monitor blood sugar closely for several days post-injection.

Procedure Details

The procedure is performed using sterile technique and fluoroscopic (X-ray) guidance.

What to Expect:

Preparation:

  • You will lie face down on a procedure table.
  • The skin is cleaned and numbed with a local anesthetic.

Injection Process:

  • A needle is guided into the epidural space in the lumbar spine.
  • A small amount of contrast dye confirms proper placement.
  • A corticosteroid is injected to reduce inflammation and nerve irritation.

Monitoring:

  • Your vitals are monitored during the procedure.
  • You will be observed briefly afterward before discharge.
  • The entire process typically takes 15â€“30 minutes.

Post-Procedural Instructions

  • Pain Relief Timeline: Steroids typically begin to take effect in 2â€“5 days but may take up to 2 weeks.
  • Activity: Resume light activity the next day. Avoid strenuous activity for 24â€“48 hours.
  • Soreness: Some temporary soreness at the injection site is normal.
  • Symptom Tracking: Keep a pain diary for 1â€“2 weeks after the injection.

If successful, additional injections may be scheduled. Typically, no more than 3â€“4 injections per year are performed in a given spinal region.

Post-Procedure Care

  • Rest for the remainder of the day.
  • Monitor for increased pain, dizziness, or signs of infection.

What are the risks and side effects?

Risks and side effects are minimal, and serious complications are rare. We take every precaution to ensure your safety. Potential risks may include but are not limited to:

  • Vasovagal response (fainting or dizziness)
  • New or increased pain
  • Infection
  • Bleeding
  • Permanent skin changes
  • Allergic or unexpected drug reaction with minor or major consequences
  • Unintended spinal or nerve injury

What if I am pregnant?

There are serious potential risks to an unborn fetus when exposed to X-ray imaging. If there is any chance you may be pregnant,

please let us know and postpone the procedure until pregnancy has been ruled out.
