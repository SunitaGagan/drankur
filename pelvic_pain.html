Pelvic Pain

Musculoskeletal Causes of Lower Back, Hip, and Sitting Pain

Overview

Pelvic pain is a broad term that refers to discomfort arising from the lower back, buttocks, hips, or tailbone. While pelvic pain is often thought of in gynecologic or gastrointestinal terms, many patients actually suffer from musculoskeletal causes such as joint inflammation, muscle strain, or nerve irritation.

At Restore Spine and Pain, we specialize in identifying and treating the orthopedic and neurologic sources of pelvic painâ€”particularly those related to repetitive strain, postural dysfunction, auto injuries, and work-related stress on the pelvis and lower spine.

Common Symptoms

  • Pain in the buttocks, hips, or groin
  • Difficulty sitting for prolonged periods
  • Pain when rising from a seated position
  • Lower back stiffness or pelvic instability
  • Radiating pain down the leg (sciatica-like)
